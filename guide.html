Come out to Sendaya!

You could die on the way. If you're in a frigate, shuttle, or pod, you'll probably be fine. The best way to experience eve is to dive in head first. You don't have that much to lose at this point anyway. Just make sure you have a clone (and if you did the tutorial missions, it would have walked you through this). Be sure to read the insta-dock section.

Insta-dock/undock

Stations are large. Often, right clicking a station and selecting warp to 0 still won't put you quite within range of the station dock. It can put you as far as 10km out, which is plenty of time for someone to lock onto you and blow you up. Similarly, when you undock and select "warp to...", it takes a moment for your ship to align to that place before you warp. Again, this is enough time for someone to lock you and blow you up.

Fortunately, there are rather simple ways around this. To access these menus, just right click an empty area of space. It can be advisable to make your own insta-dock and undock bookmarks as well. Often, people will know where our corporate undock bookmarks are set and wait there for you. This is why there are so many options in the insta-undock bookmarks for Sendaya. Just pick one at random (or better yet, make your own). Be sure to dock at the station as soon as you land at the insta-undock bookmark.

Don't worry about hauling your stuff

Frankly, your stuff weighs more than it's worth paying someone to carry it. Leave it all behind besides the ship you're flying in and some ammo. If you find you really need it later, you can always go back for it.
If you really know what you're doing, and you're certain you want stuff hauled, see our hauling page and associated FAQ

Do not, I repeat, do not use corp mail.

We have several thousand people in the corp, if everyone spammed corp mail for every little thing it would turn into a mess very quickly.
